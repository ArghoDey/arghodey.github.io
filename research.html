Overview

I build perception and decision systems for autonomous driving that stay accurate and affordable to run. A recurring theme in my work is closing the gap between state-of-the-art BEV models and the modest GPUs available in practice — through denoising attention, efficient multimodal fusion, and memory-guided temporal reasoning.

Interests

  • Bird's-Eye-View (BEV) scene segmentation & perception
  • Multimodal sensor fusion (camera + radar)
  • End-to-end autonomous driving
  • Attention mechanisms & efficient deep learning
  • Computer vision and medical image analysis

Selected Projects

UniFusion — Unified De-Noise Attention & Memory-Guided BEV Framework Ongoing

Master's thesis · Supervisor: Prof. Yunfei Yin

A unified BEV framework that integrates multimodal perception, temporal reasoning, and planning for efficient and reliable autonomous driving. It fuses camera and radar with lightweight attention and memory mechanisms to deliver robust scene understanding and decision-making on resource-constrained hardware, directly targeting sensor noise, spatial misalignment, computational cost, and temporal instability. Evaluated on the nuScenes benchmark.

ECABEV — Enhanced Channel Attention BEV Published

First author · Computers, Materials & Continua

A denoise-enhanced channel attention mechanism that fuses camera and radar under tight GPU-memory budgets. Using global average and max pooling to filter noise while keeping discriminative features, plus a bilinear-interpolation layer normalization and a scalable cross-entropy loss, ECABEV reaches state-of-the-art IoU with a lightweight ViT-B/14 backbone at 224×224 resolution. TA-SMAD — Temporal-Aware Semantic Memory-Gated Framework Under review

First author · IEEE TNNLS

A semantically guided, memory-aware BEV framework that unifies perception, temporal reasoning, and planning into a single representation, rather than treating each stage in isolation — for robust end-to-end autonomous driving.
